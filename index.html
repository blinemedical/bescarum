Discrete Mean & Variance

  • Discrete Mean - the average of a discrete random variable \(X\) a.k.a. expected value of \(X\) $$E(X) = \mu_{x} = \sum_{i=1}^n {x_{i} P(x_{i}) }$$
  • Discrete Variance - a measure of how much the probability mass is spread out around \(\mu\). $$Var(X) = E((X- \mu )^{2}) = \sum_{i=1}^n p({x_{i})(x_{i}- \mu )^{2} }$$

Discrete Standard Deviation

  • Discrete Standard Deviation \(\sigma\) - a measure of the spread, expressed in the same units as the expected Value $$\sigma = \sqrt{Var(X)}$$ $$Var(X) = \sigma^2$$

3. Bayesian Statistics

Bayes' Theorem

Muliplication rule: \(P(A \cap B) = P(A|B) * P(B)\)
$$P(B|A) = \frac{P(A|B)P(B)}{P(A)}$$
$$P(B|A)P(A) = P(A \cap B) = P(A|B)P(B)$$
$$P(B|A)P(A) = P(A|B)P(B)$$

3. Bayesian Statistics

Bayes’ Theorem Again!

$$P(\mathcal{H}|\mathcal{D}) = \frac{P(\mathcal{D}|\mathcal{H})P(\mathcal{H})}{P(\mathcal{D})}$$
$$P(Hypothesis|Data) = \frac{P(Data|Hypothesis)P(Hypothesis)}{P(Data)}$$
$$Posterior = \frac{Likelihood \times Prior}{Evidence}$$

Bayes’ Example

2 out of every 1000 people have the X gene. There is a test for it that is 99% accurate. You've tested positive.
Hypothesis: Do you have the mutant X gene?

  • \(Likelihood = P(\mathcal{D}|\mathcal{H}) = 0.99\)
  • \(Prior = P(\mathcal{H}) = 0.002\)
  • \({Evidence = P(\mathcal{D}) = 0.99(0.002) + 0.01(0.998) = 0.01196}\)
$$P(\mathcal{H}|\mathcal{D}) = \frac{0.99\times0.002}{0.01196} = 0.166$$

3. Bayesian Statistics

  • In other words, the probability of having the foo gene is 0.002
  • What is the probability that a person has the foo gene given that they test positive?
  • Hypothesis: H = person has the foo gene
  • Data: D = the test is positive
  • Prior - probability of a positive test if you have the gene
  • Likelihood - probability of having the gene
  • Evidence - a true positive test for those who have the gene, and a false positive for those who don't have it
## Base Rate Fallacy The probability that a person who tests positive actually has the gene is surprisingly low, despite the test being 99% accurate.
